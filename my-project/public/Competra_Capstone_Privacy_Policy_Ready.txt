1. ABOUT THIS PRIVACY POLICY

Competra is an academic capstone project developed by Fred Niyonshuti, a Bachelor of Science in Software Engineering student at African Leadership University in Kigali, Rwanda.

Project title:
Design and Development of a Skills Gap Analysis Tool for ICT TVET Graduates in Kicukiro District, Rwanda.

Competra is a web-based competency tracking and assessment platform designed to help ICT TVET graduates assess their competencies, identify skills gaps, compare their performance with applicable Rwanda TVET Board competency standards, and receive personalised learning recommendations.

This Privacy Policy explains how Competra collects, uses, stores, protects, shares, and deletes personal data during the capstone pilot, testing, evaluation, and demonstration periods.

Competra is an academic prototype and is not a separate legal company. Fred Niyonshuti is the project owner, developer, researcher, and primary privacy contact for the capstone pilot. African Leadership University is the academic institution associated with the project but is not automatically responsible for Competra’s day-to-day data processing unless it formally adopts, hosts, or controls the system.

This Privacy Policy is designed to reflect Rwanda’s Law No. 058/2021 of 13 October 2021 relating to the protection of personal data and privacy.

8. COMPETENCY SCORING AND AUTOMATED PROCESSING

Competra automatically calculates competency results using the following assessment weights:

- Practical task score: 50%;
- Quiz or theory score: 20%;
- Portfolio evidence score: 20%; and
- Self-assessment score: 10%.

The final competency score is calculated as follows:

Final Competency Score =
(Practical Task Score × 0.50) +
(Quiz Score × 0.20) +
(Portfolio Score × 0.20) +
(Self-Assessment Score × 0.10)

The skills gap is calculated by comparing the final competency score with the applicable competency benchmark.

Skills Gap = Competency Benchmark Score − Final Competency Score

A negative skills-gap value is treated as zero.

Competra may classify the result as:

- No Gap;
- Very Low Gap;
- Low Gap;
- Moderate Gap; or
- High Gap.

The platform may use rules or an artificial-intelligence service to recommend skills, courses, videos, practical exercises, or learning resources.

Automated results are intended for competency awareness and learning support. They do not guarantee employment, admission, certification, graduation, or professional competence. Competra will not use an automated score as the sole basis for a legal or similarly significant decision.

A user may request:

- An explanation of the result;
- Correction of inaccurate input data;
- Recalculation of the score; or
- Human review by the project owner or an authorised organisation representative.

Competra may use carefully selected service providers for:

11. DATA RETENTION

Competra uses the following capstone retention schedule:

- Account and profile data: retained while the account is active and deleted within 12 months after the capstone pilot ends or after an approved deletion request.
- Assessment results: retained until capstone evaluation, grading, correction, and any academic appeal period are completed, then deleted or anonymised within 12 months.
- Identifiable survey and interview responses: retained until the capstone report is accepted and any required academic review is completed, then deleted or anonymised within 12 months.
- Uploaded portfolio and academic evidence: retained only for assessment and validation, then deleted within 90 days after the final assessment or earlier upon an approved request.
- Temporary repository and source-code copies: removed after automated analysis or within 30 days at the latest, unless a longer period is necessary to investigate a technical or academic-integrity issue.
- Authentication and security logs: retained for up to 12 months.
- Support communications: retained for up to 12 months after the issue is resolved.
- System backups: removed through the normal backup cycle within 90 days after deletion from the active system.
- Anonymised and aggregated research findings: may be retained in the final capstone report and related academic presentations because they do not directly identify individual users.

Data may be retained longer only when required by law, an authorised academic investigation, a security investigation, or the establishment or defence of a legal claim.

- Password hashing;

- Role-based access control;
- Restrictions on administrator permissions;
- HTTPS encryption in transit;
- Secure database configuration;
- Input validation;
- Rate limiting;
- Audit and security logging;
- Secure password-reset procedures;
- Environment variables for secrets;
- Dependency and vulnerability checks;
- Isolated execution of submitted code where enabled;

No online system is completely secure. Users must protect their passwords, avoid sharing accounts, sign out from shared devices, and avoid submitting secret credentials in source code.

2. Identify the affected data and users;
3. Reduce the risk of further harm;
4. Document the incident;

Suspected incidents should be reported immediately through Competra’s Contact or Support feature or through the official academic communication channel used for the capstone project.

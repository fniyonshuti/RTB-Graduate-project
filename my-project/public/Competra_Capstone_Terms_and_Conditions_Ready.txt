7. ASSESSMENTS AND SCORING

Competra may assess competency using:

- Practical tasks;
- Quizzes or theory tests;
- Portfolio evidence;
- Academic competency records;
- Self-assessments; and
- Applicable competency benchmarks.

The current weighted scoring method is:

- Practical task score: 50%;
- Quiz or theory score: 20%;
- Portfolio evidence score: 20%; and
- Self-assessment score: 10%.

The final competency score is calculated as follows:

Final Competency Score =
(Practical Task Score × 0.50) +
(Quiz Score × 0.20) +
(Portfolio Score × 0.20) +
(Self-Assessment Score × 0.10)

The skills gap is calculated by comparing the final competency score with the applicable competency benchmark:

Skills Gap = Competency Benchmark Score − Final Competency Score

A negative skills-gap result is treated as zero.

Competra may classify a result as:

- No Gap;
- Very Low Gap;
- Low Gap;
- Moderate Gap; or
- High Gap.

The weights, benchmarks, classifications, and assessment rules may be updated to improve the capstone methodology or align the platform with approved competency standards. Material changes affecting an active assessment should be communicated where reasonably possible.

Assessment results are educational indicators. They are not official qualifications or certifications unless an authorised institution separately confirms them through its approved procedures.

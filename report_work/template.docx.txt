Daily Water Usage Tracking Accuracy
Graph Type: Bar chart

2. AR/VR Game for Autistic Students
Improvement in Emotion Recognition Accuracy
Problem Autistic students often face challenges in social interactions, communication, and sensory processing, which can make it difficult for them to engage in traditional classroom settings. They may struggle to recognize social cues, interpret emotions, and manage sensory overload in real-life interactions.
Graph Type: Line graph
Description: A line graph displaying students' accuracy in identifying emotions before and after using the game. The x-axis represents testing sessions, and the y-axis shows accuracy percentage, ideally showing an upward trend.
Time Spent in VR Social Scenarios
Graph Type: Bar chart
Description: A bar chart showing the time students spend engaging with different social scenarios (e.g., "Greeting Others," "Making Eye Contact") over sessions, suggesting increased comfort in more complex scenarios over time.
Sensory Tolerance Improvement
Graph Type: Stacked bar chart
Description: A stacked bar chart showing students' tolerance levels in various sensory environments (e.g., "Quiet," "Moderate Noise," "Crowded"). Each section within a bar represents time tolerated before feeling overstimulated, ideally growing as students progress.

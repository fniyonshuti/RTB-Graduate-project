. Capstone Report Submission Rubric
4. Capstone Report Submission Rubric
Criteria	Ratings	Pts
This criterion is linked to a Learning OutcomeAbstract
Abstract content provided, and includes all key elements
3 to >2.0 pts
Mastery
It contains a summary of all the work detailing; the introduction, objectives, methodology used, findings, conclusions, and recommendations.
2 to >1.0 pts
Proficient
The summary of the research work done is not complete
1 to >0 pts
Fail
A very weak abstract or missing
3 pts
This criterion is linked to a Learning OutcomeIncorporation of feedback
4 to >3.0 pts
Mastery
Demonstrates exceptional responsiveness to feedback, thoughtfully integrating all suggestions to enhance the clarity, coherence, and overall quality of the report.
3 to >2.0 pts
Proficient
Demonstrates good responsiveness to feedback, incorporating most suggestions to improve the report.
2 to >0.0 pts
Proving proficient
Demonstrates some responsiveness to feedback, integrating selected suggestions with room for further improvement.
0 pts
Fail
Demonstrates limited responsiveness to feedback, with minimal or ineffective incorporation of suggestions.
4 pts
This criterion is linked to a Learning OutcomeFormatting/delivery/discourse
4 to >3.0 pts
Mastery
The report adheres to all formatting guidelines, demonstrating professionalism and attention to detail. All the preliminary pages are clear and arranged in their proper order [numbering different from the main chapter pages]. Visual elements (tables, figures, etc.) are used effectively to enhance understanding.
3 to >0.0 pts
Proficient
The report adheres to most formatting guidelines, with minor inconsistencies. All preliminary pages are clear but not arranged in their proper order. Visual elements are present and support the text.
0 pts
Fail
The report lacks proper formatting, hindering readability and professionalism. The cover page needs to be included, or more than half of the preliminary pages need to be included. Visual elements are absent or detract from the content.
4 pts
This criterion is linked to a Learning OutcomeSystem implementation & testing
4 to >3.0 pts
Mastery
Provides a clear, detailed, and well-structured description of the implementation process, tools, and technologies used. Screenshots and source code are included with clear explanations and analysis.
3 to >2.0 pts
Proficient
Provides a comprehensive overview of the implementation process, including relevant tools and technologies. Screenshots and source code are present with adequate explanations.
2 to >0.0 pts
Proving proficient
Provides a basic overview of the implementation, but lacks depth or clarity in explaining tools, technologies, or code snippets.
0 pts
Fail
Implementation description is vague or incomplete, lacking essential details or explanations of tools, technologies, or code.
4 pts
This criterion is linked to a Learning OutcomeResults & discussion
4 to >3.0 pts
Mastery
Presents a thorough and insightful analysis of the results, connecting them to the research questions and project objectives. Discusses implications, limitations, and potential impact.
3 to >2.0 pts
Proficient
Presents a well-organized analysis of the results, linking them to the research questions. Discusses key findings and implications.
2 to >0.0 pts
Proving proficient
Presents a basic analysis of the results, but lacks depth or critical evaluation. Discussion of implications is limited.
0 pts
Fail
Results analysis is superficial or incomplete, failing to connect findings to research questions or discuss implications.
4 pts
This criterion is linked to a Learning OutcomeConclusions & recommendations
3 to >2.0 pts
Mastery
Student offers clear, concise, and well-supported conclusions that directly address the research questions. Provides thoughtful and actionable recommendations for future research and development.
2 to >1.0 pts
Proficient
Student offers clear conclusions that summarize key findings. Provides relevant recommendations for future work.
1 to >0.0 pts
Understanding
Student offers basic conclusions but may lack specificity or direct connection to research questions. Recommendations are general or limited.
0 pts
Fail
Student’s conclusions are vague or unsupported. Recommendations are absent or irrelevant.
3 pts
This criterion is linked to a Learning OutcomeOverall writing Mechanics [clarity, coherence, & organization]
4 to >3.0 pts
Mastery
The report is exceptionally well-written, demonstrating clarity, conciseness, and logical flow. Ideas are presented in a cohesive and organized manner, facilitating easy comprehension.
3 to >2.0 pts
Proficient
The report is well-written and organized, with a clear presentation of ideas. Minor improvements in clarity or coherence may be possible.
2 to >0.0 pts
Proving proficient
The report demonstrates basic organization and clarity but may contain grammatical errors or inconsistencies in flow.
0 pts
Fail
The report lacks clarity, coherence, and organization, hindering comprehension and overall effectiveness.
4 pts
This criterion is linked to a Learning OutcomeRelevant literature
3 to >2.0 pts
Mastery
Demonstrates a deep understanding of the relevant literature, critically analyzing and synthesizing key concepts to support the research and findings.
2 to >0.0 pts
Proficient
Demonstrates a good understanding of the literature, incorporating relevant concepts to support the research.
0 pts
Fail
Demonstrates a basic understanding of the literature, but may lack depth or critical analysis.
3 pts
This criterion is linked to a Learning OutcomeReferences
4 to >3.0 pts
Mastery
Student utilizes a wide range of credible and relevant sources, demonstrating thorough research. All in-text citations adhere perfectly to the required citation style. The reference list is accurately formatted, showcasing attention to detail and adherence to style guidelines.
3 to >2.0 pts
Proficient
Student utilizes some relevant sources, demonstrating adequate research. In-text citations sometimes adhere to the required citation style, with minor inconsistencies. The reference list is well-formatted, with minor inconsistencies or errors.
2 to >0.0 pts
Proving proficient
Student utilizes some relevant sources but may lack depth or variety. Citation may contain inconsistencies or errors. The reference list demonstrates basic formatting but may contain inconsistencies or errors that hinder clarity.
0 pts
Fail
Student utilizes limited or irrelevant sources, demonstrating inadequate research. Citations are inconsistent or absent. The reference list lacks proper formatting or is absent, significantly impacting clarity and professionalism.
4 pts
This criterion is linked to a Learning OutcomePlagiarism
2 to >1.0 pts
Mastery
The report is entirely original, with no instances of plagiarism. All sources are properly attributed.
1 to >0 pts
Developing
The report contains instances of plagiarism, with inadequate paraphrasing or quoting without proper attribution or direct copying of text.
2 pts
